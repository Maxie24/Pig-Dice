P I G - D I C E - G A M E

Pig, is a simple dice game from 1945 and is played by many rule variations. It has a couple of versions including Pass The Pigs, Pig Dice2 and Skunk Pig. Pig Dice is commonly used as a math concept to teach and emphasise on probability.


G a m e p l a y

  • Each player should repeatedly roll the dice untill either a 1 is rolled or the player decides to hold
  • If a player rolls a 1, they score nothing and it becomes the next players turn.
  • What if the player rolls another number? the numbers are added to their turn total and the player continues
  • If a player chooses to hold, their turn total is added to their score, and it becomes the next players turn

    • --The first player to score 100 or more points wins.
    For example, the first player, Winnie, begins a turn with a roll of 5. Winnie could hold and score 5 points, but chooses to roll again. Winnie rolls a 2, and could hold with a turn total of 7 points, but chooses to roll again. Winnie rolls a 1, and must end her turn without scoring. The next player, Max, rolls the sequence 4-5-3-5-5, after which he chooses to hold, and adds his turn total of 22 points to his score.
